Q: Which margin ratio do investors focus on most when comparing companies?
EBITDA Margin is the most widely used for cross-company comparisons because it strips out non-cash charges (D&A) and the effects of financing structure (interest, taxes). This makes companies with different capital structures or tax situations comparable on an apples-to-apples basis.

What If: If DSO jumps from 65 to 90 days, what happens to Nova's cash position?
Nova would need ~$46M more cash tied up in receivables: [(90−65)/365] × $672.4M = $46.1M. That's $46M less cash available for share buybacks, R&D investment, or acquisitions — a real cost even though it doesn't show up on the P&L.

Open Discussion

Nova trades at ~20.7x EBITDA. A direct competitor trades at 8x EBITDA. What are 3 possible explanations?
1. Nova has higher growth expectations — analysts forecast faster revenue growth.
2. Nova has a stronger competitive moat — its process control technology is mission-critical with high switching costs.
3. Nova operates in a more profitable niche (metrology/process control) with software-heavy revenue.
4. Scarcity premium — few comparable pure-play companies exist, so investors pay up for exposure.
